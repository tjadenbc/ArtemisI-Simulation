Faithfulness guardrail

Artemis I as-flown — no forward contamination from Artemis II / III

This audit grades how faithfully the sim re-flies Artemis I specifically — the first, UNCREWED flight of SLS Block 1 + ICPS + Orion on the DRO profile (LC-39B 2022-11-16 → Pacific 2022-12-11). The codebase is built for multi-mission reuse (Artemis II, III, and beyond), so the standing hazard is now FORWARD: later-mission hardware, trajectories, crewed systems, and — since Artemis II flew Apr 2026 — later flight DATA must not contaminate the Artemis I reference. Every shared component (and any AII/AIII port) is screened by a nature-vs-artifact test.

Nature — shared across all Artemis

Gravity fields (Earth J2–J6, GRAIL lunar), ephemerides, time standards (ΔT/TT), atmosphere physics, SRP, the integrator + reference frames. The same universe every Artemis flies through → the best available model wins and is common infrastructure.

Artifact — per-mission, never flows between missions

Mission profile (I: DRO · II: free-return lunar flyby · III: NRHO + HLS landing), vehicle config, guidance/nav targets, hardware constants + masses, dispersion magnitudes, failure rates, recovery zones, and the SUCCESS DEFINITION. Each mission keeps its own provenance-tagged config; an AII/AIII value must never bake into the AI model (or vice versa).

1Success definition stays UNCREWED. Artemis I success = SLS delivers Orion AND Orion returns & splashes intact — there is NO crew-survival model. A crewed AII/AIII survival layer must be mission-gated and must NOT alter the AI success definition or the AI definitive (AI stays uncrewed by construction).
2Mission profile stays the DRO. Artemis II's free-return lunar flyby and Artemis III's NRHO + powered lunar landing are DIFFERENT trajectories; their targets/phases (flyby geometry, NRHO insertion, descent) must not leak into the AI DRO targeting — the baked Phase-C / DRO constants are Artemis-I-specific.
3Vehicle config is per-mission. Artemis I is Orion + ICPS with no landing hardware; Artemis III adds the HLS (Starship) + lunar-surface systems (and Block 1B / EUS arrives on later flights). Those masses, thrust/Isp, and staging must never overwrite the AI ICPS + Orion ledger.
4Failure rates + dispersions stay AI-config-sourced. The AI rates are for the uncrewed Block-1 config (heritage + PRA + foreseeable). Artemis II flew successfully (Apr 2026) and III will fly, but their EMPIRICAL data is a different vehicle/mission and must NOT retroactively "correct" the AI rates — new-mission flight data informs the NEW mission's model only.
5As-planned doctrine, AI-scoped. The AI nominal targets the Artemis I flight PLAN; AI as-flown deviations (the weather-moved Guadalupe splash vs the planned off-San-Diego zone) live in the AI dispersion. AII/AIII plans and deviations are their own — do not conflate across missions.
6Imported-material screening. Material imported from another mission's model passes this same nature-vs-artifact test before adoption — nature ports, artifact stays home.
7Improvements & flaws flow as NOTIFICATIONS — for consideration, not silent edits. The wall is on silently baking a later mission's artifact VALUE into an earlier one — NOT on knowledge. If a later Artemis (or Apollo) finds an improvement or a flaw APPLICABLE to an earlier mission — a shared-infrastructure bug, a better nature-class model, a logic error, or a technique that genuinely fits the earlier config — it MUST be surfaced for that mission's consideration. The earlier mission then screens it by the nature-vs-artifact test, verifies against its own code/record, and adopts only if it truly applies. Silence is the failure mode BOTH ways: don't contaminate, but don't sit on a fix either.
